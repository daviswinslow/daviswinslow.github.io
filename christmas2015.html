Graduated from The New School

I did it! This May I finished up my master's degree in Urban Policy at The New School. While there I got to work on some interesting projects, including some research for the Washington State Institute for Public Policy, organizing a student-led campaign to divest the University's endowment from fossil fuels, and working as a teacher's assistant for a class on sustainable systems at the Parsons School of Design. And one whole person on the internet thought my class project was worthy of inclusion in a collection of resources for placemaking and urban design...

Mom and Dad, Alex, and Grandma Ann all came to New York to celebrate, along with the locals Scott and Margaret.

Officially Employed

And I have a job! After an internship during grad school I started full-time at Foundation Center as a Research Assistant, which means I do some data analysis to prepare reports on the philanthropic sector (thrilling reads...) and I run a couple of websites focused on specific issues in the field (BMAfunders & WASHfunders). My office is in the Financial District adjacent to the helipad used by VIPs when they visit the city (the left photo is of us watching during the Pope's visit), and I get to work with some great people who like each other enough to drink a beer together at the end of the week (right photo).
